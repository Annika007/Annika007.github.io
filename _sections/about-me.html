I'm Ziyou — a Peking University student in EECS, but honestly? I'm more than just code and circuits.

They call me "atypical." I call it being whole.

I love things that don't belong together: code and poetry, algorithms and sunsets, precision and the beautiful mess of being human.

Now I'm falling into embodied AI — this strange, beautiful field where we try to give machines a body, a place in the world.

I'm not sure what I'm building yet. Not just robots. Maybe a career. Maybe a self. Maybe just a life I won't regret living.

I don't know what I'm becoming yet. But I know I want to become it fully — unafraid of challenges, gentle with myself, shining quietly in the things I love.

This space holds what I've made and what I've loved.

Some frames are empty. They're waiting.

So am I.
